Isabella is a competent leader. She was the first chair and division chief of the dulcimer part of her elementary school orchestra. She set a role model for my contemporaries in the orchestra. In second grade, she became the class delegate. She was also the teacher’s assistant at Tsinghua University Primary and High school in a variety of subjects including math, English, computer applications and biology. Every morning, she checked attendances and the punctuality of assignment turn-ins from her classmates. She organized homework memos, helped her teachers to correct the assignments, and handed the work back to the students. She become more independent and competent through the process of her work.



Isabella relishes the arts of life. She plays the flute, the recorder and many other instruments for fun. She is keen on baking and she is a member of THIS’s baking club. She appreciates animals and plants. She likes playing chess, has passed level 12 in international chess and level 5 in Go in Chinese Chess Association. She values human connection, and has joined and made new friends at the THIS Chinese debate club. These all help her obtain a comfortable study-life balance.



Isabella's top hobby

Isabella loves performing arts. Ever since four years old, she has kept practicing the piano and passed the level 9 test at the Central Conservatory of Music. Playing the piano ignites her desire to discover, excel, warm and encourage. Her family and friends are elated and cheered up by her music. As she grew up, she conceived the dream to combine her talent in arts with her creativity and foster a more hospitable community.

Isabella's current favorite food

Tuna Otoro. Otoro is the name of the fattiest part of a tuna. Otoro has a characteristic sophisticated taste and a distinctive fat marbeling which makes the pieces to melt in your mouth. The area on the abdomen near the gills is called harakami, and the center of the abdomen is called haranaka. Otoro is located between the harakami and haranaka.

Different Types of Cheese

>>1. Fresh Cheese<<

Fresh cheese will not be further processed. After the milk is coagulated, water will be removed, and there will be a milk flavor. The shelf life is short, so it is better to eat it as soon as possible.

This type of cheese include cottage, mozzarella (oooooh MOZZARELLA), cream (mmmmmm) and queso fresco.

>>2. White Mold Cheese<<

The skin of white mold cheese has a layer of white fungal villi, which can be left or removed when eating.

Examples are La Brie and La Camembert.

>>3. Blue Mold Cheese<<

Blue mold cheese is also called blue cheese, with beautiful blue lines on the surface. The stronger the contrast with the white part, the better the maturity. It is wet, fragile and tastes strong. It can be eaten alone or made into pizza or sandwich.

Examples are gorgonzola, blue stilton and roquefort.

>>4. Goat Cheese<<

As the name implies, goat cheese is made from goat milk. It is small in size, various in shape and slightly sour in taste. Goat cheese is divided into plastered and non plastered cheese. The taste of non plastered cheese is usually smooth and delicious.

Examples are picadon, crottin and rocamadour.

>>5. Hard Cheese<<

Hard cheese is usually large and thick, with high salinity, and has a relatively long mature period and storage time. This is also the main type of cheese that often appears in the cartoon Tom and Jerry.

Examples are emmenthal and cheddar (yumyum CHEDDAR).

>>6. Washed Rind Cheese<<

In the process of making washed cheese, the skin needs to be constantly cleaned with salt water. During this process, mold will ferment, so the cheese has a special smell and a very strong taste.

Examples are limburger and époisses de bourgogne.
